Social Impact Portal

I worked on an app concept with Stephanie Bond, the Product Designer at ECP, as a User Experience Design Intern. I helped mostly with user researching, information architecture, and user experience.

The Problem

There was a need to increase community involvement in the social impact sector of Boston, MA. No exclusive platform existed where organizations and community members can post about or directly become aware of events supporting social impact.

The App

This portal aims to appeal to a younger audience. The landing page invites anyone to join the social impact community in Boston. All the visitor of the portal has to do is choose categories and the information regarding social impact events will be filtered and catered to their interests.

As the visitor scrolls, the current events are shown without signing up to the portal. After, there is an option where visitors and users of the portal can input their feedback and what improvements they would like to see.

When a visitor signs up to be a user of the portal, he or she has the ability to post an event relevant to Social Impact and also change filters for searching events.

Re-designed BPM Website

BPM partnered with Startup Institute Boston to host the first Ideahack. My cohort was placed into teams and I was one of the web designers in my IdeaHack team.

The Challenge

My IdeaHack team focused on developing a feasible plan for BPM to execute without spending much capitol since they are a non-profit. My challenge was to make changes to the already existing website and improve the User Experience.

The landing page now has a banner with the information every customer looks for like hours of operation and location.

While I re-designed the BPM website I considered myself a new customer and as a new customer I did not know what "The Kitchen" was. In the original landing page there are many links to events happening at "The Kitchen" and no explanation about what "The Kitchen" is. To remediate, I added a picture of the BPM kitchen and a catchy tagline that would prompt new customers to click on the picture link. The link would take the customer to a page where all the information relevant to The Kitchen is located.

BPM uses social media to build it's client base. I dedicated this part of the landing page to all of their social media postings.

Financial Budgeting App

CoinUp aims to help college freshmen learn financial literacy through the gamification of budgeting.

The Challenge

The second IdeaHack at Startup Institute was led by Tom Hughes the former Creative Director of the Macintosh. Mr. Hughes challenged the cohort to ideate, design, and present an app concept that was interesting to each of the teams. I presented my app concept to my team and College Pocket became CoinUp.

The left screen is the homepage of CoinUp, once the roulette wheel has been spun and the daily budget challenge has been chosen then the user is taken to the right screen. The right screen showcases the daily budget challenge, how much money the user has spent, and also the passage of time is seen as a circular meter.

The profile page shows the records of when the user has accomplished or failed a daily budget challenge and the total amoint of Coins the user has earned.

After the 24 hours are up one of these two screens will pop up depending if the user kept or exceeded the daily budget challenge.
